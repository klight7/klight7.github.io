Inspiration

Observations by the Roadside
Near the subway station by my home in China, street vendors, often elderly, gather daily to sell homegrown vegetables. When I was around eight, my sister and I also ran a small night market stall for a week. My family still laughs about how I fell asleep on the stall cloth one night, though I barely remember it. In high school, I once sold school newspapers, managing just 11 copies after a morning of effort. These experiences make me wonder: with e-commerce and modern trends, is it harder for small vendors to thrive? Their business often depends on trust and human connection, yet they face biases about hygiene, quality, and reliability. Inspired by their struggles, I thought of designing a game exploring their world, powered by interactions with Large Language Models (LLMs).

Sci-Fi Inspiration and the Birth of NutriSynth
As a sci-fi enthusiast, Iâ€™ve always been fascinated by futuristic worlds where technology redefines humanity. Films like Blade Runner 2049, with its synthetic protein farms, sparked my imagination. Observing real-world tensions between tradition and innovation inspired me to envision a future where artificial nutrition dominates, reflecting society's shifting values.
This led to the creation of NutriSynthâ€”a synthetic product promising efficiency and perfection but dividing society over its impact on culture and human connection. Drawing from these themes, I set my game in a near-future world shaped by this tension, exploring how progress and tradition collide in everyday life.

Reflection
Through the playtest and my own experience developing the game, Iâ€™ve identified key areas for improvementâ€”ranging from UI clarity and pacing to technical challenges with AI behavior, TTS/STT integration, and Unity rendering. While some issues were already anticipated and partially addressed before testing, others became more apparent only after observing player interactions.

i. TTS & STT Integration Issues:
The TTS system (ElevenLabs API + LLM-generated dialogue) sometimes ran slowly, affecting response timing; STT (OpenAI Whisper) occasionally failed to transcribe words correctly, leading to misinterpretations.
Potential Improvement: Explore fine-tuning STT models for better transcription accuracy.

ii. LLM (AI Dialogue) Stability & Content Issues
The LLM's responses can be too friendly, vague, or unclear, likely due to OpenAIâ€™s default settings prioritizing friendliness and safety over precision. This sometimes leads to confusing in-game interactions.
Potential Improvements:
1. Refine prompt engineering for clearer, more structured responses.
2. Implement stricter conversation flow to prevent AI from drifting off-topic.
3. Test alternative model parameters or add a fallback response system for better consistency.

iii. Unity Rendering & Lighting Optimization Issue:
Struggled with Unity's lighting settings, affecting the visual quality sometimes.
Potential Improvement:
Exploring more on URP/HDRP, baked lighting, and post-processing effects to create a more polished and optimized visual experience.

iv. AI & Navigation Pathfinding Issues:
Customers took too long to arrive, making the game feel slow. A reset point in Unity Behavior was added before the playtest to reposition distant customers. However, itâ€™s still not fully effective.
Potential Improvement:
Further fine-tuning pathfinding logic to ensure smooth pedestrian movement without abrupt resets. Implementing a waiting queue system for customers, ensuring a steady flow while maintaining immersion.
